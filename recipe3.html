Recipe Information


Treacle tarts are a classic British dessert featuring a buttery shortcrust pastry filled with a sweet, gooey mixture of treacle (or golden syrup), fresh breadcrumbs, and a hint of lemon zest. The tart is typically baked until the filling is set, resulting in a delightful contrast between the crisp pastry and the soft, sweet filling. Treacle tarts are famously known as Harry Potter's favorite dessert, making them a beloved treat among fans of the series.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour and 5 minutes

Serves: 10 people

Dietary Information: Contains gluten, dairy, and eggs. Nut free. Suitable for vegetarians.

Ingredients

For the Pastry

  • 250g plain flour All-purpose flour can be used as an alternative.
  • 1/2 tsp fine salt Can be omitted if not preferred.
  • 140g unsalted butter, cubed Margarine can be used as a substitute.
  • 3 tbsp icing sugar Granulated sugar can be used as an alternative.
  • 2 medium egg yolks Flaxseed meal mixed with water can be used as an egg substitute.
  • 2-3 tbsp cold water Use as needed to bring the dough together.

For the Filling

  • 400g golden syrup Light corn syrup can be used as an alternative.
  • 1 ball stem ginger, finely chopped Fresh ginger can be used as a substitute.
  • 1 lemon, zested Lemon juice can be used for flavor.
  • 2 medium eggs, lightly beaten Flaxseed meal mixed with water can be used as an egg substitute.
  • 100g fine fresh white breadcrumbs Can use crushed digestive biscuits as an alternative.

Instructions

  1. Sieve the flour and salt into a large bowl. Add the butter and rub together with your fingers to a fine breadcrumb-like texture.
  2. Stir through the icing sugar, then quickly add the egg yolks and 2 tbsp water, mixing swiftly with a cutlery knife to combine. Form into a ball (add another tbsp water if you need to), wrap and chill for 30 mins.
  3. Roll out to the thickness of a pound coin, and line a 22cm fluted tart tin with the pastry, leaving an overhang. Return to the fridge for 30 mins.
  4. Heat the oven to 200C/180C fan/gas 6. Put a baking sheet into the oven to heat up. Line the pastry case with baking parchment and baking beans, then put in the oven on the baking sheet, and bake for 15 mins.
  5. Remove the parchment and bake for a further 10 mins or until the pastry is golden brown. Leave the pastry to cool before trimming off the overhanging edges with a small serrated knife.
  6. Lower the oven temperature to 160C/140C fan/gas 4. Combine the golden syrup, ginger, ginger syrup, lemon, eggs, and breadcrumbs in a bowl, briefly whisking everything together until combined.
  7. Carefully pour the filling into the pastry case and put in the lower part of the oven to bake for 35-40 mins or until the filling is just set.
  8. Remove from the oven and leave to cool down for 20 mins before serving with ice cream or thick clotted cream.
