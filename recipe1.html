Recipe Information


Profiteroles are exquisite French pastries made from choux pastry, which is a light and airy dough created from butter, water, flour, and eggs. These delightful treats are typically filled with rich custard or whipped cream and are often topped with a luscious chocolate glaze. The contrast between the crispy exterior and the creamy filling makes profiteroles a favorite dessert for many.

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 20 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Serves: 6 people

Dietary Information: Contains gluten, dairy, and eggs. Nut free. Suitable for vegetarians.

Ingredients

For the Profiteroles

  • 50g unsalted butter, cubed Can substitute with margarine for dairy-free.
  • 2 tsp caster sugar Granulated sugar can be used as an alternative.
  • 75g strong white flour, sifted All-purpose flour can be used as a substitute.
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten Flaxseed meal mixed with water can be used as an egg substitute.
  • 300ml double cream Coconut cream can be used for a dairy-free option.
  • few drops vanilla extract Vanilla essence can be used as an alternative.

For the sauce

  • 50g cocoa powder Carob powder can be used as a caffeine-free alternative.
  • 175g caster sugar Granulated sugar can be used as an alternative.

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 220C/200C fan/gas 7.
  2. Put the butter and 2 tsp of the caster sugar in a saucepan with 150ml water. Place the pan over a low heat until the butter and sugar have melted, then bring to the boil.
  3. Take off the heat, add the flour all at once and beat energetically with a wooden spoon until the dough comes away from the sides of the pan.
  4. Leave to cool for 5 mins, then beat in the eggs bit by bit until you have a stiff, glossy mixture.
  5. Rinse two baking trays with cold water, shaking off any excess so they are slightly damp (this helps the pastry to rise).
  6. Using 2 teaspoons, spoon blobs of the mixture onto the baking trays, then place in the oven and cook for about 18-20 mins until well risen and brown.
  7. Remove the profiteroles from the oven and cut a small slit in the base of each one so they don’t collapse. Cool on a wire rack.
  8. When they’re cold, whip the cream lightly until just holding its shape. Sweeten to taste with remaining sugar and a few drops of vanilla extract.
  9. To make the sauce, sift the cocoa powder into a bowl. Put the sugar in a pan with 100ml water and warm over a low heat until dissolved. Bring to the boil, cook for 1 min, then pour over the cocoa powder and stir well until smooth.
  10. Return the sauce to the pan, cook for 1 min then set aside for 15 mins before drizzling over the profiteroles.
